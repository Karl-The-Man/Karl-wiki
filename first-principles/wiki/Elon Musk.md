# Elon Musk

CEO of [[SpaceX]], [[Tesla]], and [[The Boring Company]]. Studied physics and computer science; describes himself as "hyperrational" and "obsessed with truth." Self-taught in rocketry through reading and consulting experts.

## Philosophical Framework

Musk's foundational motivation is curiosity about the nature of the universe. He calls this the "[[Philosophy of Curiosity]]"—a commitment to expanding consciousness (human and digital) so that humanity can eventually answer questions about the meaning of existence. He studied physics because it "attempts to understand the truth of the universe" and also studied information theory to understand logic. *(elon-musk-on-first-principles.pdf)*

He considers [[First Principles Thinking]] the most powerful cognitive tool available, derived from the physicist's method. He explicitly contrasts it with [[Reasoning by Analogy]], which he says dominates everyday thinking and produces only incremental improvement. For important problems—especially in technology and engineering—he insists on decomposing to fundamental truths and reasoning upward.

## Decision-Making Methods

- **[[Idiot Index]]**: Ratio of finished-product cost to raw-material cost. Used at SpaceX to identify parts with inefficient manufacturing.
- **Magic wand number**: The theoretical cost floor if you could rearrange atoms for free. Sets the target.
- **[[Thinking in Limits]]**: Scale a variable to an extreme to test assumptions (e.g., "Would this part still be expensive at a million units per year?").
- **[[Obsession with Truth]]**: Actively guards against [[Wishful Thinking]]; assumes the team is "losing, even when it looks like we might win."

## On Learning

Advocates voracious, self-directed learning. Read the *Encyclopedia Britannica* as a child. Views knowledge as a [[Semantic Tree of Knowledge|semantic tree]]—trunk and branches (fundamentals) before leaves (details). Prefers reading over lectures due to higher "data rate." Learned rocketry from library books and borrowed engine manuals before founding [[SpaceX]]. *(elon-musk-on-first-principles.pdf)*

> "Most people can learn a lot more than they think they can. They sell themselves short by not trying."
