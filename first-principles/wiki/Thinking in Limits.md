# Thinking in Limits

A reasoning tool from physics that [[Elon Musk]] applies broadly: take a variable and imagine scaling it to an extreme (very large or very small) to reveal what's actually possible and what's merely a current constraint. *(elon-musk-on-first-principles.pdf)*

## Method

> "Take a particular idea and imagine scaling it to a very large or very small number. How do things change?"

By pushing to extremes, you separate fundamental constraints (physics, thermodynamics) from incidental ones (current regulations, today's tooling, production volume).

## Applications

### Manufacturing
Ask: "If our volume was a million units per year, would it still be expensive?" If yes, volume isn't the problem — the design or process is fundamentally flawed. If no, focus on scaling production. At sufficient volume and manufacturing skill, cost asymptotically approaches raw material value plus IP licensing fees. *(elon-musk-on-first-principles.pdf)*

### Tunneling ([[The Boring Company]])
- **Depth limit:** There's no real limit to how many levels of tunnel you can have — the deepest mines far exceed the tallest buildings. Cities are 3D but roads are only 2D; tunnels can fix this.
- **Diameter limit:** Shrink from 26–28 ft to 12 ft → area drops 4x → cost drops ~4–5x.
- **Machine uptime limit:** Current machines work 50% of the time (alternating between boring and reinforcing). Continuous boring + reinforcing → 2x.
- **Power limit:** Current machines are far from thermal/power limits. Increasing power → another 2–5x.
- Combined theoretical improvement: 8–40x from ~$1B/mile baseline. *(elon-musk-on-first-principles.pdf)*

### Product Design
Two-directional thinking:
1. **Bottom-up:** What can we build with the tools we have?
2. **Top-down (platonic ideal):** What is the perfect arrangement of atoms for this product? Then figure out what tools, methods, or materials are needed to get atoms into that shape.

The "theoretically perfect" product is a moving target — as knowledge grows, the definition shifts — but it anchors the design process to the optimum rather than to familiarity. *(elon-musk-on-first-principles.pdf)*
