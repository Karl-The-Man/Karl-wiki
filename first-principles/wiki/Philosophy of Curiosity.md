# Philosophy of Curiosity

[[Elon Musk]]'s self-described foundational philosophy: the commitment to understanding the nature of the universe by expanding the scope and scale of consciousness.

> "This is the foundation of my philosophy: I am curious about the nature of the universe."
> *(elon-musk-on-first-principles.pdf)*

## Core Argument

1. We don't know the meaning of life — "yet."
2. To find out, we must expand humanity and consciousness (both biological and digital).
3. Total collective consciousness = number of conscious beings x average consciousness per being.
4. Expanding both terms increases our capacity to ask the right questions of the universe.
5. The question is harder than the answer — properly framing the question is the real challenge.

Musk draws on [[Douglas Adams]]'s *The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy*: "The universe is the answer. We need to figure out what questions to ask."

## Implications

This philosophy motivates Musk's ventures:
- **[[SpaceX]]**: Making humanity multiplanetary expands the scope of consciousness and protects against existential risk.
- **[[Tesla]]**: Sustainable energy removes a bottleneck on civilization's longevity.
- **Neuralink** (mentioned in source citations): Digital intelligence as a way to increase the "scale" of consciousness.

## On Consciousness

Musk speculates about the nature of consciousness itself: "Is everything conscious or is nothing conscious? Maybe it's just degrees of consciousness or concentrations of consciousness." He finds it remarkable that hydrogen gas, given 13.8 billion years, "became sentient." *(elon-musk-on-first-principles.pdf)*

## Civilizational Timescale

Written language is ~5,000 years old. If civilization lasts a million more years, we are "barely even at the beginning." Future humans will view us as "ancient ancients, like cavemen." This timescale argument reinforces the urgency of preserving and expanding civilization. *(elon-musk-on-first-principles.pdf)*
