# Semantic Tree of Knowledge

A metaphor [[Elon Musk]] uses for how to structure learning: master the trunk and big branches (fundamental principles) before moving to the leaves (details), so that the details have something to hang on to. *(elon-musk-on-first-principles.pdf)*

> "It is important to view knowledge as a semantic tree. Make sure you understand the fundamental principles (the trunk and big branches) before you get into the leaves (the details), then there is something for them to hang on to."

## Relationship to First Principles

The semantic tree metaphor is the *learning* counterpart to [[First Principles Thinking]] as a *reasoning* method. Both emphasize the priority of fundamentals over specifics. You cannot reason from first principles about a domain if you haven't first internalized the trunk-level knowledge of that domain.

## Musk's Learning Practice

- Read voraciously as a child, including the entire *Encyclopedia Britannica* by age ~10
- Prefers reading over lectures (higher "data rate": thousands of bits/sec vs. hundreds)
- Recommends broad exploration of the "knowledge landscape" before specializing
- Learned rocketry from library books and expert consultations, not formal aerospace education
- Credits strategy video games (*Civilization*) with teaching him about technology trees and the stages of knowledge development

> "How can you know what you're really interested in if you're not at least doing a broad, light exploration of the knowledge landscape?"
> *(elon-musk-on-first-principles.pdf)*
