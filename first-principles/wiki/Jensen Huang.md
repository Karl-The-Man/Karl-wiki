# Jensen Huang

Founder and CEO of [[NVIDIA]]. Born in Taiwan, moved to the US as a child. Started his career as a dishwasher at Denny's, then worked at [[LSI Logic]] as an engineer before co-founding NVIDIA in 1993 with Chris Malachowsky and Curtis Priem.

## First Principles Practice

Huang applies [[First Principles Thinking]] as a continuous reinvention prompt: learn how something works, then ask "given today's conditions, how would I reinvent this from scratch?" He applies this to:

- **Technology strategy** — The OpenGL manual at Fry's Electronics saved NVIDIA when their original 3D approach became incompatible with Direct 3D. "How hard can it be? You're probably one textbook away."
- **Organizational design** — Designed NVIDIA's flat structure (50 direct reports) from first principles rather than copying conventional hierarchies. See [[Organizational First Principles]].
- **Market creation** — Selects problems based on importance, not market size. "If we didn't do it, would it happen without us?" See [[EOIFS]].
- **Crisis navigation** — When NVIDIA dropped 80% in market cap, returned to core beliefs: "Did physics change?" See [[Gut-Check Resilience]].

## Leadership Philosophy

- **Empowerment through reasoning**: Shows people *how* he reasons through problems — ambiguous, incalculable, or scary ones — to empower independent decision-making across the organization.
- **Radical transparency**: Believes the CEO should have the most direct reports because those people need the least management. Information hoarding as a power source is antithetical to his model.
- **No task is beneath me**: Traces this directly to his Denny's years. "You can't show me a task that's beneath me."
- **Service orientation**: "My job is to create the conditions by which you can do your life's work."

## Key Relationships

- Co-founded NVIDIA with Chris Malachowsky and Curtis Priem (both from Sun Microsystems)
- Funded by Don Valentine of [[Sequoia Capital]] on recommendation from Wilf Corey ([[LSI Logic]] CEO)
- Collaborated with [[Andrew Ng]] and [[Alex Krizhevsky]] on early deep learning work
