# Wishful Thinking

A cognitive bias that [[Elon Musk]] identifies as the primary enemy of sound decision-making: the tendency to believe things are the way you *wish* them to be, filtering out disconfirming information. *(elon-musk-on-first-principles.pdf)*

## How It Manifests

- In business: underestimating difficulty, ignoring red flags, mistaking desire for evidence
- In personal life: confusing perseverance on a viable idea with chasing an unrealistic dream
- In engineering: assuming a design will work without checking against physics
- Universally: "You want things to be the way you wish them to be, so you tend to filter out information you shouldn't."

## Musk's Warning Signs

- Something "feels too easy"
- Something "doesn't quite make sense"
- Confident assertions without axiomatic grounding

## Countermeasures

Musk's toolkit for guarding against wishful thinking includes:
- [[Obsession with Truth]] — the dispositional antidote
- [[First Principles Thinking]] — forces grounding in verifiable truths
- Seeking negative feedback from all sources
- Assuming "we're losing, even when it looks like we might win"
- Rigorous self-analysis to distinguish belief from evidence

> "Do you have the right fundamental axioms, or truths? Are they relevant? Are you making the right conclusions based on those truths? That's the essence of critical thinking."
> *(elon-musk-on-first-principles.pdf)*
