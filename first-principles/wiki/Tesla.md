# Tesla

Electric vehicle and energy company. [[Elon Musk]] used [[First Principles Thinking]] to challenge the assumption that EV batteries would always be prohibitively expensive.

## The Battery Cost Breakthrough

Industry consensus: battery packs would always cost ~$600/kWh. Musk's first-principles decomposition:
1. What are batteries made of? Cobalt, nickel, aluminum, carbon, polymers, steel can.
2. What do those materials cost on the London Metal Exchange? ~$80/kWh.
3. Conclusion: "We just need to think of clever ways to take those materials and combine them into the shape of a battery cell."

This analysis — an application of the [[Idiot Index]] concept — proved that the cost barrier was a manufacturing problem, not a physics problem. *(elon-musk-on-first-principles.pdf)*
