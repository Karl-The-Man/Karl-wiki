# Latent Demand

A product strategy concept applied by [[Boris Cherny]]: when non-target users jump through hoops to use a product not designed for them, it's a signal to build a product specifically for them.

## The Claude Co-Work Example

Non-engineers at [[Anthropic]] — finance, sales, data scientists, DevRel — were already using [[Claude Code]] despite it being designed for engineers. This organic adoption signaled that there was unmet demand for agentic AI capabilities among non-technical users.

The response was [[Claude Co-Work]]: the same agentic capabilities in a more accessible interface with additional safety layers. Its growth trajectory was steeper than Claude Code's initial trajectory — "an instant hit" — confirming the latent demand hypothesis.
