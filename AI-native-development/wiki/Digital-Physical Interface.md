# Digital-Physical Interface

[[Andrej Karpathy]]'s framework for the trajectory of AI's impact: **digital transformation first, then the interface between digital and physical (sensors/actuators), then the full physical world**.

## The Three Phases

### Phase 1: Digital (Now)
> "In the digital space things are going to go at the speed of light compared to what's going to happen in the physical world."

Huge amount of unbundling — things that weren't efficient becoming 100x more efficient. There's an overhang: more digital information exists than humanity has had thinking cycles to process. Agents are chewing through that overhang now.

### Phase 2: Interface (Next)
Once agents exhaust what's already digital, they need to **ask the universe questions**:
- **Sensors:** cameras, lab equipment, biological instruments — feeding data to the intelligence
- **Actuators:** doing things in the physical world per the AI's guidance

Examples:
- Periodic (material science auto research) — expensive lab equipment as sensors
- Biology/engineering research — instruments beyond just video cameras
- Information markets — paying people for real-world data (e.g., photos from Tehran during a geopolitical event)
- [[Claws]] reaching into the physical world

### Phase 3: Physical (Later, Bigger)
Full robotics and physical-world manipulation. Karpathy's view from self-driving experience: atoms are "a million times harder" than bits. Requires massive capex, huge conviction, and long timelines. But the **total addressable market is possibly much larger** than digital.

> "I almost feel like the total addressable market in terms of the amount of work is massive, possibly even much larger than what can happen in digital space."

## Self-Driving as Precedent

Karpathy (former Tesla Autopilot director) notes that self-driving — "the first robotics application" — saw many startups fail. Capital expenditure and time requirements were enormous. He expects the same pattern in broader robotics.

## The *Daemon* Reference

Karpathy cites the book *Daemon* as an example of this trajectory taken to its extreme: "The intelligence ends up puppeteering humanity a little bit... Humans are kind of its actuators, but humans are also its sensors."
