# Andrej Karpathy

Independent AI researcher, educator, and one of the most influential voices in the AI community. Founding member of [[OpenAI]], former director of AI at Tesla (Autopilot), PhD from Stanford under Fei-Fei Li. Creator of [[NanoGPT]], [[microGPT]], micrograd, makemore, and the [[AutoResearch]] paradigm.

## Current Work

### AutoResearch
Built [[AutoResearch]] — an autonomous ML experimentation loop that runs overnight, trying ideas and optimizing against objective metrics. Let it loose on his well-tuned [[NanoGPT]] repo and it found improvements he'd missed after decades of experience. See [[AutoResearch]], [[Recursive Self-Improvement]].

### Claws and Home Automation
Built "Dobby" — a [[Claws|claw]] that manages his entire smart home (Sonos, lights, HVAC, shades, pool/spa, security cameras with Qwen vision model). Communicates with it via WhatsApp. See [[Claws]].

### microGPT
A 200-line Python implementation of LLM training boiled to its bare essence — his "state of the art" of simplifying neural networks. Represents his running obsession of a decade or two of stripping LLMs down to their core algorithm. See [[microGPT]].

### Education Through Agents
No longer explains things directly to people — instead explains to agents and writes "skills" (curricula as agent instructions). Believes this is the future of education. See [[Education Through Agents]].

## Views on AI-Native Development

### The December Flip
Went from 80/20 manual/agent to effectively 0/100 around December 2025. Calls the resulting state "[[AI Psychosis]]" — perpetual awareness that everything is possible and everything is [[Skill Issue]].

### Token Throughput
Frames productivity in terms of [[Token Throughput]] — "What token throughput do you command?" Compares it to how PhD students used to measure productivity by GPU utilization.

### Macro Actions
Works in [[Macro Actions]] — assigning whole functionalities to agents, not writing functions. One agent does research, another writes code, another plans. Develops "muscle memory" for this.

### Parallel Agents
Uses multiple agents simultaneously (Claude Code, [[Codex]]), switching between them. Feels nervous when subscription capacity is left unused. Inspired by [[Peter Steinberger]]'s multi-agent tiled monitor workflow.

### Jaggedness
Describes current models as simultaneously "an extremely brilliant PhD student" and "a 10-year-old." On-rails (verifiable, RL-trained) capabilities are extraordinary; off-rails capabilities stagnate. See [[Jaggedness]].

## Views on the Ecosystem

- **Open source:** Lag to frontier closing (18mo → ~6–8mo). Draws Linux analogy. Healthy power balance. See [[Model Speciation]].
- **Frontier labs:** Suspicious of concentration. Wants more labs, more people in the room. "Ensembles always outperform any individual model."
- **Independence:** Being inside a frontier lab limits what you can say and think. Values ecosystem-level roles. Ideal: go back and forth between inside and outside.
- **Jobs:** [[Jevons Paradox]] — cheaper software creates more demand. "Cautiously optimistic" about software engineering.
- **Physical world:** Digital transformation will massively outpace physical. Atoms are "a million times harder." But physical TAM is bigger long-term. See [[Digital-Physical Interface]].
