## [2026-04-13] ingest | Thin Harness, Fat Skills (Garry Tan)
Ingested Garry Tan's (YC president) long-form post on agent architecture. Proposes "thin harness, fat skills" — the inverse of Harrison Chase's emphasis on harness thickness. Five definitions: skill files (reusable markdown procedures as method calls), the harness (thin, ~200 lines), resolvers (context routing tables), latent vs. deterministic (the design boundary), and diarization (structured synthesis from documents). Illustrated with a detailed YC Startup School case study (6,000 founders, enrichment → matching → self-rewriting skills). Creates the wiki's central architectural tension: Chase says the harness is the key layer and memory lock-in is the risk; Tan says the harness should be thin and value lives in portable skills.
