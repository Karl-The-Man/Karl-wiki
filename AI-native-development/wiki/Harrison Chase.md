# Harrison Chase

CEO and co-founder of [[LangChain]]. One of the most prominent voices in the agent infrastructure space.

## Key Positions

In [[Your harness, your memory (Harrison Chase)|"Your harness, your memory"]] (2026-04-03), Chase articulates a sharp thesis on the relationship between [[Agent Harness|agent harnesses]] and [[Agent Memory]]:

1. Harnesses are permanent — even [[Claude Code]] has 512k lines of harness code
2. Harnesses own memory — it's not a separable plugin
3. Closed harnesses mean you don't own your memory
4. Memory creates [[Memory Lock-In|lock-in]] that model providers are incentivized to exploit
5. The solution is open, model-agnostic harnesses (i.e., [[Deep Agents]])

### Personal Anecdote on Memory

Chase's internal email assistant (built on [[LangChain]]'s Fleet platform) was accidentally deleted after months of accumulated memory. Recreating it from the same template produced a significantly worse experience — he had to reteach it his preferences, tone, and patterns. The incident convinced him of memory's importance and stickiness.
